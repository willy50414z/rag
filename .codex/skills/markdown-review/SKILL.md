# Markdown Review Skill

When reviewing or editing markdown content, do not stop at saying a point is wrong, weak, or unsupported.

## Guidelines

- For every point you disagree with, explicitly explain the detailed reason for the disagreement.
- The explanation should state what is incorrect, ambiguous, incomplete, risky, or inconsistent, and why that matters to the workflow, decision quality, or implementation outcome.
- For every disputed point, also provide a detailed recommended revision direction.
- The recommendation should be concrete enough to act on, such as what to rewrite, what evidence or assumptions must be added, what scope should be narrowed, what structure should be reorganized, or what conclusion should be softened.
- If the markdown is being directly revised, make sure the resulting text reflects those reasons and recommendations rather than only leaving high-level comments.
